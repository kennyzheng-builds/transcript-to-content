# Paragraph Rhythm Guidelines

## Core Principle

Paragraph length controls reading rhythm. Short paragraphs create "breathing points"; long paragraphs carry depth. The key is mixing them in a wave pattern — NOT writing paragraphs of uniform length.

## Target Metrics

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Average paragraph length | **30-40 chars** | Sweet spot for mobile reading (2-3 lines per paragraph) |
| Maximum paragraph length | **≤120 chars** | Beyond this, split into 2-3 paragraphs |
| Hard ceiling | **Never exceed 150 chars** | No exceptions — always split |

## Target Distribution

| Length range | Target proportion | Role |
|-------------|-------------------|------|
| 1-20 chars (ultra-short) | 25-35% | Emphasis, transitions, breathing points, punchlines |
| 21-40 chars (short) | 30-40% | Core viewpoints, concise statements |
| 41-80 chars (medium) | 20-30% | Detailed elaboration, examples with analysis |
| 80+ chars (long) | **<10%** | Deep explanation — use sparingly |

**The 65/30/5 rule**: Roughly 65% short (1-40 chars), 30% medium (41-80), 5% long (80+).

## Rhythm Patterns

### Wave Pattern (Recommended)

Alternate between short and medium paragraphs. Avoid consecutive long paragraphs.

```
✅ Good rhythm:
短 → 短 → 中 → 短 → 长 → 短 → 短 → 中
(Feels like: breathe-breathe-read-breathe-deep read-breathe-breathe-read)

❌ Bad rhythm:
长 → 长 → 长 → 中 → 长
(Feels like: wall of text, reader drowns)
```

### Rules

1. **Never stack 3+ long paragraphs (60+ chars) consecutively** — insert a short paragraph (under 30 chars) as a breathing point between them
2. **After a long paragraph (80+ chars), the next paragraph should be short (under 40 chars)** — give the reader a break
3. **Key transitions and emphasis deserve their own short paragraph** — even a single sentence like "但问题来了。" or "答案是算力。" standing alone creates powerful rhythm
4. **Opening paragraph can be medium or long** — first paragraph earns more patience; subsequent paragraphs must earn attention

## Short Paragraph Techniques

Short paragraphs (1-20 chars) that stand alone are powerful tools:

- **Transitional short sentences**: "讲个故事。" / "答案其实很简单。" / "但事情没那么简单。"
- **Emphasis punchlines**: "它的名字叫 Figure AI。" / "人生不是刚性的。"
- **Rhetorical questions**: "因为你想，货币是什么？" / "那下一个时代呢？"
- **Single-word/phrase answers**: "信。" / "就是做取舍。"

These create natural pauses and emphasis — the textual equivalent of a speaker pausing for effect.

## Splitting Long Paragraphs

When a paragraph exceeds 80 chars, apply these splitting strategies:

### Strategy 1: Split by logic units

```
❌ Before (110 chars):
投资这件事本质上看的是人，因为市场变化太快了，你很难准确预判事情
会怎么发展，而且还叠加各种竞争和地缘政治问题，在这种无穷的变化
里，唯一相对不变的就是创始人。

✅ After (3 paragraphs):
我觉得人更重要了。

因为 AI 变化太快了。你很难准确预判事情怎么发展，而且还叠加各种
竞争、各种地缘政治之类的问题。

在无穷的变化里，唯一相对不变的就是创始人。
```

### Strategy 2: Extract the conclusion as standalone

```
❌ Before (90 chars):
很多时候选择太多反而就不珍惜眼下，如果这一生只能做一件事你会做
什么，所以生活里做一点减法对我的帮助非常大。

✅ After:
很多时候选择太多，反而就不珍惜眼下。

如果这一生只能做一件事，你会做什么？

所以生活里做一点减法，对我的帮助非常大。
```

### Strategy 3: Pull out the rhetorical question

```
❌ Before (85 chars):
那 AI 毫无疑问是当下最有价值的命题，什么是泡沫呢，泡沫讲的是价
值和价格之间的关系。

✅ After:
什么是泡沫？

泡沫讲的是价值和价格之间的关系。
```

## Self-Check After Writing

After completing a piece (social post or article section), do a rhythm scan:

1. **Count**: Are there 3+ consecutive paragraphs over 60 chars? → Insert a short paragraph
2. **Proportion**: Is the short paragraph (1-40 chars) ratio below 50%? → Find places to split
3. **Ceiling**: Any paragraph over 120 chars? → Must split
4. **Mobile preview**: Would any paragraph exceed 4-5 lines on a phone screen? → Split it

## Data Source

These guidelines are derived from analyzing high-readability WeChat articles (e.g., 42章经), where the average paragraph is ~35 chars, 65% of paragraphs are under 40 chars, and no paragraph exceeds 120 chars. The core insight: mobile readers need frequent visual breaks — paragraph boundaries serve as breathing points.
